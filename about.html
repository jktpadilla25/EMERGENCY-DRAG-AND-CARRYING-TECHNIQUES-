Emergency Carrying and Drag Techniques

NSTP Lesson on Emergency Response

Introduction

Emergency carrying and drag techniques are important rescue skills taught in the NSTP program. These techniques are used during accidents, disasters, and other emergency situations where a person may be injured or unable to move on their own. When a victim is in danger, rescuers must know the proper way to move them without causing further injury. Learning these techniques helps students become more prepared to respond during emergencies and assist people who need immediate help.

In many situations such as fires, earthquakes, road accidents, or building evacuations, a person may become unconscious or physically weak. Because of this, rescuers must know how to safely carry or drag the victim to a safer location. These methods are taught so that both the rescuer and the victim remain protected while moving away from danger.

Carrying Techniques

Carrying techniques are used when a rescuer needs to lift and transport a victim from one place to another. These techniques require proper body posture, balance, and coordination. If done incorrectly, the rescuer may suffer injuries such as back strain, while the victim may experience more pain or damage to their body.

Some common carrying techniques include the fireman's carry, cradle carry, and two-person carry. The fireman's carry is often used by trained rescuers because it allows them to carry the victim over their shoulders while keeping one arm free for balance. The cradle carry is used when the victim is lighter and can be held safely in the rescuer's arms. Meanwhile, the two-person carry requires teamwork between two rescuers to lift and move the victim more safely.

These carrying techniques are especially useful when the victim cannot walk or when the rescuer needs to move them quickly away from danger. However, it is important to assess the situation first before deciding which method to use.

Drag Techniques

Drag techniques are used when lifting the victim is not possible or when the environment is too dangerous for carrying. In emergency situations such as smoke-filled rooms, narrow hallways, or areas with debris, dragging may be the safest and fastest option to move the victim.

Some common drag techniques include the blanket drag, clothes drag, and ankle drag. The blanket drag is performed by placing the victim on a blanket or cloth and pulling it along the ground. This technique helps support the body while moving the victim. The clothes drag involves holding the victim's clothing behind the shoulders and pulling them carefully across the ground. The ankle drag is sometimes used when the rescuer needs to move the victim quickly and no other method is available.

Although dragging is helpful in emergencies, rescuers must still be careful and avoid dragging victims over rough surfaces whenever possible to prevent additional injuries.

Importance of Learning These Skills

Learning emergency carrying and drag techniques is very important for students, especially those who are part of the NSTP program. These skills help students become more responsible, aware, and prepared during emergencies. Instead of panicking during accidents, trained individuals can respond calmly and assist those who need help.

These skills also promote teamwork and cooperation. Many rescue techniques require more than one person to assist the victim safely. By practicing these methods, students develop trust and learn how to communicate effectively with others during stressful situations.

More importantly, knowing how to properly rescue someone can save lives. In many cases, immediate action during the first few minutes of an emergency can prevent serious injuries or even death.

Conclusion

Emergency carrying and drag techniques are valuable life-saving skills that everyone should learn. Through proper training and practice, students can develop the confidence and ability to respond during real-life emergencies. These techniques not only help protect victims but also ensure that rescuers remain safe while performing their duties.

By learning these skills in the NSTP program, students become more prepared to serve their communities and assist others during disasters or accidents. Preparedness, knowledge, and compassion are key qualities that every responsible citizen should develop.
